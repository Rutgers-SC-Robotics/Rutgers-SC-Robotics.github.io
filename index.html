| ABOUT SOCRATES

Integrating Robotics With Social Sciences

SOCRATES is the Rutgers Robotics NRT awarded September 2020, creating a new vehicle for graduate training and convergence research that integrates robotics with social and behavioral sciences.

Program Goals

Training socially cognizant roboticists by integrating STEM and social sciences to address the future of work and human-robot interaction.



| Certificate Program

Graduate Certificate In Socially Cognizant Robotics

The Certificate program trains a new type of reflective practitioner, through the convergence of the socially aware technologists and the technology-aware social scientist. The certificate is comprised of a three-course sequence developed by the SOCRATES NRT faculty.

Robotics and Society (16:332:640 and cross-listings)

Explores how technology and society interact, covering ethics and social impacts of robotics

Socially Cognizant Robotics (16:332:590 and cross-listings)

Explores foundations and state-of-the-art developments in robotics.

Design Methods in Socially Cognizant Robotics (16:332:595 and cross-listings)

Hands-on experience through practical projects with interdisciplinary teams.

Chalk Talk Series

Monthly Discussions And Workshops

Chalk-Talk Series brings together trainees, participants, and faculty advisors for monthly team meetings, faculty talks, and reading group discussions. These sessions include workshops on writing skills, presentation preparation, CV building, job search strategies, technical interview preparation, and responsible research practices. The series connects new students with current research areas and senior NRT trainees with newer cohorts. Regular feedback is provided through rubric-based assessments, focusing on communication skills development and fostering a collaborative community across diverse disciplinary backgrounds.
